# Student Violations Reference Guide

## Cordova Catholic Cooperative School - SAO Office

This document outlines the types of violations tracked in the SAO E-Record System, along with their descriptions and typical consequences.

---

## 1. Academic Dishonesty

**Description:** Cheating, plagiarism, or unauthorized collaboration

**Examples:**
- Copying answers during exams or quizzes
- Submitting someone else's work as your own
- Using unauthorized materials during tests
- Collaborating on individual assignments without permission
- Fabricating data or sources in research papers
- Using AI tools without proper attribution

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Zero on assignment, parent conference
- Second Offense: Failing grade for the course, suspension
- Third Offense: Possible expulsion, permanent record notation

---

## 2. Behavioral Misconduct

**Description:** Defiance, disrespect to staff, profanity, or disrupting the learning environment

**Examples:**
- Refusing to follow teacher instructions
- Talking back or showing disrespect to staff
- Using inappropriate language or profanity
- Disrupting class activities
- Excessive talking or noise-making
- Throwing objects in class
- Leaving class without permission

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Verbal warning, detention
- Second Offense: Parent conference, suspension
- Third Offense: Extended suspension, behavior contract

---

## 3. Physical Violence & Aggression

**Description:** Fighting, assault on students or staff, or intimidation

**Examples:**
- Physical fights between students
- Pushing, shoving, or hitting others
- Threatening physical harm
- Assault on teachers or staff members
- Intimidating or threatening behavior
- Gang-related activities

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Immediate suspension, parent conference, possible police involvement
- Second Offense: Extended suspension, behavior contract
- Third Offense: Expulsion, legal action

---

## 4. Bullying & Harassment

**Description:** Physical, psychological, or sexual harassment, including cyberbullying

**Examples:**
- Repeated teasing or name-calling
- Spreading rumors or gossip
- Social exclusion or isolation
- Physical intimidation
- Sexual harassment or unwanted advances
- Cyberbullying via social media or messaging
- Sharing inappropriate images or messages
- Stalking or persistent unwanted contact

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Suspension, mandatory counseling, parent conference
- Second Offense: Extended suspension, restraining order if needed
- Third Offense: Expulsion, possible legal action

---

## 5. Substance Use & Possession

**Description:** Possession or usage of drugs, alcohol, or tobacco/vaping products

**Examples:**
- Possession of illegal drugs
- Using or being under the influence of alcohol
- Smoking or vaping on school grounds
- Possession of drug paraphernalia
- Distributing or selling substances
- Prescription medication misuse

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Suspension, mandatory drug testing, counseling referral
- Second Offense: Extended suspension, substance abuse program enrollment
- Third Offense: Expulsion, police involvement

---

## 6. Vandalism & Theft

**Description:** Damaging school property or stealing personal property

**Examples:**
- Defacing school walls, desks, or equipment
- Breaking windows or doors
- Damaging textbooks or library materials
- Stealing from lockers or classrooms
- Taking school property without permission
- Graffiti on school premises
- Destroying landscaping or outdoor facilities

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Restitution, detention, community service
- Second Offense: Suspension, full restitution, parent conference
- Third Offense: Extended suspension or expulsion, legal action

---

## 7. Weapons Violation

**Description:** Possession of firearms, knives, or other dangerous items

**Examples:**
- Bringing firearms to school
- Carrying knives or bladed weapons
- Possession of explosives or fireworks
- Bringing replica weapons
- Possession of pepper spray or tasers
- Any item used as a weapon

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Immediate expulsion, police involvement, legal prosecution
- Zero tolerance policy - automatic expulsion

---

## 8. Attendance Issues

**Description:** Chronic absenteeism, truancy, skipping class, or tardiness

**Examples:**
- Unexcused absences
- Skipping classes
- Leaving school grounds without permission
- Chronic tardiness (3x late, 5x late)
- Forging excuse notes
- Excessive absences (3x absent)

**Typical Consequences:**
- 3x Late: Warning, parent notification
- 5x Late: Detention, parent conference
- 3x Absent: Mandatory parent meeting, attendance contract
- Continued Issues: Possible retention, truancy court referral

---

## 9. Policy Violations

**Description:** Violating dress codes, using prohibited technology, or gambling

**Examples:**
- Dress code violations (inappropriate clothing)
- Unauthorized use of mobile phones in class
- Using electronic devices during exams
- Gambling or betting on school premises
- Violating internet usage policies
- Unauthorized photography or recording
- Bringing prohibited items to school
- Violating parking regulations

**Typical Consequences:**
- First Offense: Warning, confiscation of items, parent notification
- Second Offense: Detention, parent conference
- Third Offense: Suspension, loss of privileges

---

## Violation Reporting Process

### For Students:
1. Incident occurs
2. Teacher/staff reports to SAO
3. Student called to SAO office
4. Investigation conducted
5. Parents notified
6. Consequences determined
7. Follow-up meeting scheduled

### For Parents:
1. Receive notification (email/phone)
2. Review violation details
3. Schedule meeting with SAO
4. Discuss consequences and action plan
5. Sign acknowledgment form
6. Monitor student progress

---

## Appeal Process

Students and parents have the right to appeal any disciplinary action:

1. Submit written appeal within 3 school days
2. Appeal reviewed by SAO Director
3. Hearing scheduled if necessary
4. Decision communicated within 5 school days
5. Final appeal to School Principal if needed

## Support Services

The school provides support for students with violations:

- **Counseling Services**: Individual and group counseling
- **Behavior Intervention**: Customized behavior plans
- **Peer Mediation**: Conflict resolution programs
- **Parent Workshops**: Parenting and discipline strategies
- **Academic Support**: Tutoring and mentoring
- **Substance Abuse Programs**: Referrals to treatment centers

---

## Prevention Programs

CCCS implements various prevention programs:

- Character education curriculum
- Anti-bullying campaigns
- Drug awareness programs
- Conflict resolution training
- Digital citizenship education
- Peer mentoring programs

---

## Record Keeping

All violations are recorded in the SAO E-Record System:

- Date and time of incident
- Type of violation
- Description of incident
- Witnesses involved
- Action taken
- Parent notification
- Follow-up meetings
- Resolution status

Records are maintained confidentially and used for:
- Tracking patterns of behavior
- Identifying students needing support
- Generating reports for administration
- Compliance with school policies
